Oasis — "Be Here Now" Rhythm

Be Here Now (1997, Creation). The Marshall-stack wall-of-overdubs record. Standard tuning.
Era rig: Marshall JCM900 stacks (+ Matchless Superchief 120, JTM45/Bluesbreaker; Vox Conqueror in studio) Owned core: UAFX Lion '68 pushed hard → FRFR Boost: Maxon OD808 (tighten + push) Width: PS-6 detune + MXR chorus Pick: heavy, hard strum

JCM900 is a flagged gap. The Lion '68 is a 1968 Super Lead Plexi — same Marshall family but lower-gain/looser than the album's JCM900. Push Volume/Gain higher than a vintage-Plexi setting to chase it. Anti 1992 won't help (it's a Peavey 5150 voice).

The Wall — Layering the real "tone"

Overdub density · the defining technique
Track count2–4× same part
Panhard L / R (stack outward)
Take disciplinere-play, don't copy/paste
GluePS-6 detune + slow chorus
Be Here Now is overdub density first, tone second. A single pass will sound thinner than the record — that's a track-count problem, not a tone problem. Do not fix thinness by adding gain. Re-perform each layer (slightly different feel per take) rather than duplicating one track — the tiny timing/pitch differences are what make the wall move.
"There were so many guitars on it… it just became this monolithic, gigantic-sounding thing." — the Be Here Now overdub approach, paraphrased. The mass is the point.

Source-Confirmed vs. Inferred

Source-confirmed (the rig) v

  • Marshall JCM900 was the primary era/live amp; JTM45/Bluesbreaker + Matchless Superchief 120 (ordered 1997) in the stack — Ground Guitar.
  • A Vox Conqueror was prominent as the main studio amp on the album — Ground Guitar.
  • Guitars: Gibson Les Paul + Epiphone ES-355 / Riviera humbuckers — Ground Guitar.
  • Marshall stacks used across Definitely Maybe / Morning Glory / Be Here Now — Oasis Recording Info (Noel quote).
  • UAFX Anti 1992 = Peavey 5150 (not Marshall) — uaudio.com; that's why it's ruled out for this voice.

Inferred / substituted a

  • All knob positions are starting points — no published per-song settings. Dial by ear.
  • JCM900 → Lion '68: same Marshall lineage, lower gain. Closest owned amp-in-a-box; not a 1:1 voice.
  • Vox Conqueror: no owned Vox voice — Greenback cab + presence is the nearest approximation (secondary gap).
  • PRS Tremonti for the LP / semi-hollow humbucker sources — solidbody fallback.
  • OD808 & chorus values were realigned to their stated intent (the prototype had inverted numbers).
