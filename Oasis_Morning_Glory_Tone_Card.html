Oasis — "Don't Look Back in Anger" (Morning Glory era)

(What's the Story) Morning Glory? (1995, Creation). Anchor songs: "Don't Look Back in Anger," "Champagne Supernova," "Wonderwall" (electric layers). Standard tuning.
Era rig: Vox AC30 + Marshall Bluesbreaker combo (both Helix-modeled — neither owned) Drive: Ibanez TS-9 → TS808 (push, not distort) Mod: EHX Small Stone phaser → EVH Phase 90 Time: Roland Space Echo → Flashback 2 Tape Pick: heavy, hard strum

Two-Amp Setup — AC30 + Bluesbreaker Blend

Both are Helix models — you own neither amp. Run two amp blocks summed to one FRFR, roughly 50:50, tilt toward Bluesbreaker for rhythm body. Goal: amps on the verge of breakup; the TS808 sends them over the edge. Gain stays in crunch territory — not high-gain.

Source-Confirmed vs. Inferred

Source-confirmed (the rig) v

  • WTSMG rhythm parts written/recorded on an Epiphone Riviera; Sheraton the other iconic semi-hollow — The Pedal Show / Boost Guitar Pedals / Mixdown.
  • Core amps = Vox AC30 (~1964) + Marshall Bluesbreaker combo — Boost Guitar Pedals, Andertons.
  • Ibanez TS-9 Tube Screamer used almost exclusively through the '90s to push the amp front end — Mixdown, Andertons.
  • Modulation movement = EHX Small Stone phaser ("Champagne Supernova") — Guitar Chalk.
  • Delay = Roland Space Echo (studio) / Boss DD-3 (live slapback) — Mixdown.

Inferred / substituted a

  • All knob positions are starting points — Noel hasn't published per-song settings. Dial by ear from the source out.
  • AC30 & Bluesbreaker are Helix models — neither owned; FRFR ≠ real speaker in a room.
  • PRS Tremonti for the semi-hollow — solidbody fallback; airy semi-hollow character not reproducible.
  • EVH Phase 90 for the Small Stone — right device class, different sweep.
  • Flashback Tape for the Space Echo — flavor-direct, not the same machine.
