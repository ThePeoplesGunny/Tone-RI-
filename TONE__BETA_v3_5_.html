Roman numerals are key-independent. Upper = major, lower = minor. Examples in C major — apply to any key.

Diatonic Pattern — Universal (applies in any key)

I Major Tonic home · resolved · stable
ii Minor Subdominant motion · departure · setup
iii Minor Tonic softer home · color variation
IV Major Subdominant motion · leads to V or back to I
V Major Dominant maximum tension · wants to resolve to I
vi Minor Tonic relative minor · darker arrival
vii° Diminished Dominant very tense · wants to resolve upward

Pattern is fixed. Only the chord names change with the key. V always has maximum tension. I always resolves. This does not change.

The Problem TONE Solves
The hand doesn't know what the mind knows. 20 years of pentatonic muscle memory survives circular 1-4-5 progressions but fails when harmonic gravity becomes directional. The ear hears it. The mind feels it. The hand doesn't know where to go.
TONE: name what the ear knows → show the hand where to go
