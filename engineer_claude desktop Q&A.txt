What the Studio Series Actually Adds
The honest answer about pedal-amps vs. real tube amps in this circuit category:
The UAFX Lion '68 is the best Plexi-in-a-pedal commercially available. Reviewers consistently rate it close to the real thing for tone. What it doesn't fully replicate — and what every Plexi-pedal-vs-real-amp comparison surfaces — is the power amp interaction. A Plexi's defining feel comes from a non-master-volume circuit where the power tubes are doing significant work. The way the amp sags under a hard pick attack, the way it compresses as you dig in, the touch response that makes guitarists describe Plexis as "intoxicatingly responsive" — those come from real EL34s working into a real cab. The Lion approximates this; it doesn't deliver it.
For the EVH Brown Sound specifically: Eddie's tone wasn't just the circuit, it was the circuit being driven flat-out into a cab at volume. The Variac was about getting the power amp to that breakup point at slightly lower voltage. A pedal can model the EQ curve and saturation; it cannot fully model the physical cab interaction at volume. The PowerCab is a remarkable FRFR but it's not a 4x12 with EL34s actually pushing it.
So the Studio Series isn't a tonal-target mismatch — it's a fidelity upgrade on the Plexi side of your rig.

The Recommendation, Revised
If the goal is "real Plexi feel for the EVH Brown Sound + Hendrix + McCready lineage at usable volume": SV20H. It's the right circuit for the right targets.
Honest caveats on the SV20H specifically:

Non-master volume. To get the real Plexi feel you have to push the amp. 5W mode helps but reviewers consistently note it's still loud. Plan on an attenuator (Two Notes Captor X, Boss Waza Tube Amp Expander, or a passive like the Bugera PS1) — budget another $300–$700 for that.
It's a pedal platform when clean. To get gain at lower volumes without an attenuator, you'd be using a boost pedal, which somewhat defeats the purpose vs. the Lion '68.
Your existing 4x12s (EVH and Mesa) will both work. The EVH 4x12 (likely loaded with Celestion G12EVH or similar) will be more aggressive; the Mesa V30 4x12 will be tighter and mid-forward. Either is a legitimate Plexi cab.

If the goal is "Marshall in the room, single amp, most flexible": 2525H Mini Jubilee. Two channels, master volume, usable at home, covers more ground than the SV20.
If the goal is "fill the JCM800 gap specifically": SC20H — but the UAFX Enigmatic '82 ($399) is a real alternative for less money and zero added volume problems.
