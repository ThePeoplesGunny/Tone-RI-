# Lion '68 + PowerCab 212 Plus — The Honest Verdict

## The Configuration That Matters

There's a critical setup question hiding in this. The PowerCab can run in three modes: **Flat (FRFR), Speaker Modeling (12 internal speakers), or IR (third-party impulse responses)**. The Lion '68 has **internal cab modeling** (six miked cabinets, derived from the OX Amp Top Box's modeling tech) **that can be bypassed**.

**The right configuration:** Lion '68 cabs ON → PowerCab in **Flat (FRFR) mode**. This is the setup the Lion '68 was designed for. UA built the cab modeling to be the speaker stage, so the PowerCab needs to be transparent — full-range, flat-response, neutral 70Hz-20kHz. The Lion's cab models do the speaker work; the PowerCab just amplifies and reproduces what comes out of the pedal.

**The wrong configuration:** Lion '68 cabs ON → PowerCab in Speaker Modeling mode. This is "two cabs stacked." You're telling the Lion to model a Greenback, then telling the PowerCab to model a different Greenback on top of it. The result is muddy, scooped, double-filtered. Common mistake. Sounds bad.

**Alternative that also works:** Lion '68 cabs OFF (use UAFX Control app to disable) → PowerCab in Speaker Modeling mode. Now the Lion is amp-only, the PowerCab is the speaker. This works but you lose UA's high-quality cab modeling derived from the OX. Generally inferior to the first config for plexi tones specifically, since UA's Greenback/JBL D-120F mixed cab in Brown mode is the historically-correct EVH cabinet pairing.

## The Three Modes — Settings for Your Use Case

### Lead Mode (1968 Super Lead)
**For:** Hendrix, McCready, vintage-Marshall classic rock crunch. The "factory" Plexi tone.
**Settings to start:** Volume I at 5-6, Volume II at 4-5 (these jumper internally — both add gain), Treble 6, Middle 5, Bass 4, Presence 4. Bright cap ON (default).
**Cab:** Try the "67 GBack 4x12" first (Greenback 4x12 — the classic Plexi cab). The "75 GBack" or the EV12 are alternatives.
**This is the McCready / Yellow Ledbetter / Hendrix territory.**

### Bass Mode (1968 Super Bass)
**For:** Cleaner, darker, looser, more headroom. Pedal platform. Excellent for fuzz, delay, and chord work.
**Settings to start:** Volume I at 4-5, Volume II at 4, Treble 6, Middle 6, Bass 5, Presence 4. No bright cap by default.
**Cab:** Same Greenback 4x12 works well, or try the JBL D-120F (the bonus cab) for more clarity.
**This is the "Plexi as pedal platform" mode.** Less crunch, more headroom.

### Brown Mode (1968 Super Lead Variac'd to 90V)
**For:** Van Halen I-era Brown Sound. The "hot-rodded Plexi" tone. EVH 1978-1984.
**Settings to start:** Volume I at 7-8, Volume II at 5-6, Treble 6, Middle 5, Bass 4, Presence 5. Boost knob at noon (engages the EP-III preamp emulation — Eddie used an Echoplex EP-3 in front of his Plexi for additional gain). Bright cap OFF (default for Brown — it's removed in the model).
**Cab:** "Mixed Greenback / JBL D-120F 4x12" — this is Eddie's actual cab on Van Halen I. UA built this cab specifically for Brown mode. **Use this one.**
**This is the literal Brown Sound recipe.**

## Hidden Settings via UAFX Control App

These are where you fine-tune for your room and your PowerCab:

- **Room Tone:** Adjusts the modeled room ambience around the cab. For PowerCab playback in a real room, dial this DOWN (you're already in a real room — no need to add a fake one). Some users zero it out completely. Try 9-10 o'clock max.
- **Bright Cap:** Toggle on/off per mode. In Brown mode, leave it OFF (period-correct EVH spec). In Lead mode, try both — bright cap on for jangle, off for warmer breakup.
- **Ghost Notes:** UA's emulation of the subtle harmonic artifacts a real tube amp produces. Leave on.
- **Noise Gate:** Engage if you're getting hum at high gain (Brown mode especially).

## Where the Pairing IS Genuinely Limited

I'll be straight with you. Three honest limitations:

**1. The "amp in the room" feel is different.** The PowerCab in Flat mode is intentionally neutral — it reproduces what you give it accurately. A 4x12 with EL34s pushing it has a specific resonance, low-end thump, and physical presence that comes from cab construction + speaker compliance + power amp interaction. The PowerCab approximates this well in Speaker Modeling mode but in Flat mode (which is correct for Lion '68 use) it's deliberately not coloring the sound. Some players prefer the more colored "real cab" feel even if it's less accurate to the modeled amp.

**2. Picking dynamics and feel.** Real tube power amps have a specific compression/sag character when you dig in hard. The Lion '68 models this well — UA specifically calls out Brown mode's "aggressive attack and bounce" and the MusicRadar review highlighted feel and response to picking dynamics as the pedal's strength — but it's still a model. Some players can A/B and tell. Many can't, especially in mix or band context.

**3. The "headroom ceiling" is digital.** A real Plexi pushed past its limits has a specific way of distorting the input. The Lion '68 has internal limits to its modeling. In practice, this almost never matters because you'd never push it that hard. But it's a real difference.

## Where the Pairing IS Genuinely Excellent

**1. Brown Sound character at any volume.** This is the killer feature. Real EVH Brown Sound requires a 100W Plexi pushed past 7 with a Variac at 90V — that's "rattling drywall" volume even in 5W mode on a Plexi clone. Lion '68 + PowerCab gets you that *exact tonal character* at apartment volume. You cannot do this with any tube amp, no matter how much money you throw at attenuators.

**4. Already integrated with your PowerCab signal chain.** ANTI 1992, Knuckles '92 (planned), Lion '68 — all UAFX pedals into the PowerCab. One signal chain, multiple amp characters. The SV20H breaks that uniformity.
